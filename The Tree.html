The Tree

by: JOYCE KILMER


I
think
that I shall
never see. A poem
lovely as a tree. A tree
whose hungry mouth is prest.
Against earth's sweet flowing breast;
A tree that looks at God all day, And lifts her
leafy arms to pray; A tree that may in Summer wear
A nest of robins in her hair; Upon whose bosom snow has lain;
Who intimately lives with rain.
Poems are
made by
fools like
me,But
only
God
can
make
A TREE
